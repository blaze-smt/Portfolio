Stapled Hills

Stapled Hills was my second clothing brand. I saw it as a step up from infst. I wanted Stapled Hills to tell a story and have each piece connect with one another. Also wanted the brand to had to have a piece of me incorporated into it so all of the designs were based around pixel art because I grew up playing video games. this was also around the time I got into web design. I created the website design myself and paid someone to make it because I had no idea how to code with ruby on shopify. I will always love stapled hills. I was 20 years old and I really tried so hard to make everything for it look good. I paid professionals to sew on tags, screenprint, clean up my designs. I also purchased higher quality blanks from rue porter and LA Apparel. at least that was my perception of high quality at the time.

Brand Identity

There was a few elements I incorporated into Stapled Hills that made it unique. I wanted to tell a story without directly telling the story. Each graphic that was made for the brand was an addition to the consumers interpretation. From the combination of pixel art, deep meaning quotes, and otherworldly graphics. Stapled Hills was able to achieve its own identity.

I wanted a consumer to find the clothing easily and right away, while also having a unique experience. With each drop, I created a new website background to go with its theme.

Marketing

I put some more thought into the social media side of marketing with Stapled Hills. Since the following was so small I was able to easily interact with consumers directly. I was more concerned about building a cult-like following rather than making money since this was just something to do for fun. To do this I hosted giveaways on instagram stories, gave discount codes to loyal customers, and created unique graphics and videos to go with each drop. This ended up being to my benefit because I had regular consumers buying every drop.

Clothing Design Process

I had a certain process I followed to create each piece. This developed over time, but It included everything from the first ideas of the graphic to the promotional material used. First I would find references to the idea in my head. From that I would basically pixelate those references, clean them up myself, and send it to a pixel artist on Fiverr to finalize the design. I would also give the artist rough assets and drawings to create the video material as well. After the graphic side of things was done I would mockup the looks for instagram and the website.
